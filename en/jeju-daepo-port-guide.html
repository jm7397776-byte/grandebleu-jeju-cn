Daepo Port, Jeju: How to Get There & What You'll See by Sea

Seogwipo · South Coast · Jusangjeolli

Daepo Port (대포항) sits on Jeju's south coast in Seogwipo, right beside one of the island's most famous natural landmarks — the Jusangjeolli columnar cliffs. It's a small working harbour, and it's where the Grande Bleu sunset cruise sets sail. Here's how to get there and what the coast looks like once you're on the water.

How to get to Daepo Port

  • From Jeju City — about an hour by car or taxi across the island to the south coast.
  • From Seogwipo / Jungmun — only around 10–15 minutes; it's very close to the Jungmun resort area.
  • By bus — local Jeju buses serve the Daepo / Jungmun area; check the live route to the stop nearest the port.
  • By car — set your navigation to "Daepo Port" (대포항) in Seogwipo; there's parking near the harbour.

Your booking confirmation will give the exact meeting point and boarding time, which shifts through the year with the sunset.

Jusangjeolli cliffs: the neighbour

Right next to the port, the Jusangjeolli Cliffs are walls of hexagonal basalt columns formed by cooling lava — one of Jeju's signature sights. You can walk the cliff-top viewing deck before or after your cruise. But here's the thing most visitors miss: from land you see the top of the columns. From the water, you see them rise straight out of the sea, at their full height, the way they were meant to be seen.

What opens up once you're offshore

Within a few minutes of leaving the harbour the perspective drops to sea level and the coast reveals layers you can't reach on foot:

  • Wolpyeong columnar joints and hidden sea caves along the cliff base
  • Elephant Rock (Daesangbawi) — a rock formation shaped, yes, like an elephant
  • The volcanic, layered texture of the southern coastline, lit from the side at golden hour

Wild dolphins occasionally pass through these waters too — a bonus when it happens, never a promise.

Make it a half-day

Because the port is close to Jungmun, it's easy to pair the cruise with the area's other highlights — the Jusangjeolli deck, Jungmun beach, and the cafes nearby — and then finish with the sunset on the water. One hour at sea is the natural finale to a south-coast afternoon.
