Jeju Yacht for Couples: Honeymoon, Anniversary & Proposal Sailing

Romance · Sunset · Seogwipo Daepo Port

Some moments deserve a better backdrop than a restaurant table. If you're planning a honeymoon, an anniversary, or a proposal on Jeju, an hour at sea as the sun goes down is about as cinematic as the island gets — and it takes almost none of your trip's time to arrange.

Why couples choose the sunset sail

Jeju is full of romantic ideas, but most of them happen on land, in public, surrounded by other tourists. A catamaran cruise gives you something rarer: space, quiet, and a horizon that's all yours. The deck is open, the light turns gold, the coastline drifts past in silhouette — and for a little while it genuinely feels like the two of you have the sea to yourselves.

It's not about how many places you visit. It's about the one moment you'll both still describe years later.

Honeymoon: an easy highlight

Honeymooners are usually juggling a packed itinerary. The beauty of the cruise is that it's only one hour from Daepo Port, with food and drinks already included — draft beer, wine, Jeju tangerine juice and snacks — so there's nothing to plan or carry. Step aboard, pour a glass, and let the crew handle the rest.

Anniversaries & proposals: go private

For a milestone, ask about a private charter. With the boat to yourselves you can shape the timing around the exact sunset moment, keep a surprise genuinely private, and not worry about strangers in your photos. If you're planning a proposal, message the team in advance with your date and plan so they can help with the timing — the run-up to sunset is the window you want.

A few things that make the setting work so well for couples:

  • A wide, stable twin-hull deck — easy to move around, easy to get the shot
  • An indoor air-conditioned lounge if the breeze gets cool
  • Korea's only certified yacht you can board without a life jacket, so the photos stay elegant
  • A professional crew that's run the cruise 100,000+ times

Photo tips for two

  • Don't over-pose — leaning on the rail looking out to sea is the most natural frame there is.
  • Let the deck, sails and coastline into the shot for depth.
  • Start shooting 30 minutes before sunset to catch the richest light.
