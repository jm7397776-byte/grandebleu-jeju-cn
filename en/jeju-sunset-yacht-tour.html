Jeju Sunset Yacht Tour: What the Catamaran Cruise Is Really Like

Jeju Island · Seogwipo Daepo Port · Sunset Sailing

Most travellers come to Jeju with a long list — waterfalls, craters, cafes, viewpoints. But ask anyone what they actually remember, and it's rarely a checklist item. It's a moment. For a lot of our guests, that moment happens about forty minutes into the cruise, when the wind drops, nobody is talking, and the sun is sitting right on the horizon between the sails.

This is a first-hand walkthrough of the Grande Bleu sunset yacht tour — what it is, why a sunset on the water is different from one on land, and what to expect from the moment you board at Seogwipo Daepo Port.

Why a sea sunset beats a land viewpoint

Jeju has plenty of famous sunset spots, and they're beautiful. But they all share the same limitation: you're standing still, on land, looking out. The horizon is fixed, the foreground is a railing, and you're sharing it with a crowd.

On a yacht, the whole frame changes. The boat moves, the light moves across the water toward you, and the coastline slides past in silhouette. Instead of watching the sunset, you're inside it — and for photographers, couples and first-time visitors, that difference is the entire point.

It's a real catamaran, not a speedboat

Grande Bleu is a sailing catamaran — a twin-hulled boat that actually raises its sails, rather than a motorboat or jet craft. That twin-hull design matters more than it sounds:

  • A wider, flatter deck — more room to move around, relax and take photos without feeling cramped.
  • A steadier ride — far less rocking and seasickness, so older guests and children are comfortable too.
  • Open 360° views — nothing blocking the horizon when the light turns gold.

The outside is an open deck where you can stand at the bow and feel the sea air; inside there's a lounge with sofas and air-conditioning if you want to warm up. That flexibility is a big reason the cruise stays comfortable in every season.

What you'll see from Daepo Port

The cruise leaves from Daepo Port in southern Jeju, right next to the famous Jusangjeolli columnar cliffs. Within a few minutes of leaving the harbour, the view opens up in a way you simply can't get from land:

  • The hexagonal basalt columns of Jusangjeolli, seen from sea level
  • Hidden sea caves along the cliffs
  • Elephant Rock and the layered volcanic coastline

Dolphins do appear in these waters, but they're wild — we never promise them. The coastline and the light are the guaranteed stars of the show.

Safe enough to relax

For first-time sailors, trust matters more than anything. A few things worth knowing:

  • Grande Bleu is the only certified yacht in Korea you can board without wearing a life jacket.
  • A professional skipper and engineer run the boat for the entire cruise.
  • The operation has run 100,000+ times and partners with 5-star hotels on the island.

None of that is decoration — it's exactly the reassurance a nervous first-timer needs before stepping aboard.

Everything onboard is included

You don't need to bring food or drinks. The ticket already covers:

  • 🍺 Draft beer and wine
  • 🍊 Jeju tangerine juice, water, snacks and instant ramen
  • 🎣 A sea-fishing experience — you might land a local rockfish, scorpionfish or filefish

"Just show up and step aboard" is the whole idea, and it's especially welcome for international travellers who don't want to plan around yet another logistics detail.
