What's included onboard

Your ticket already covers the extras, so there's nothing to buy or carry on:

  • 🍺 Draft beer and wine
  • 🍊 Jeju tangerine juice and bottled water
  • 🍜 Snacks and instant ramen
  • 🎣 A sea-fishing experience — rockfish, scorpionfish and filefish are common local catches

How to book

You have two easy options:

  • Klook — the simplest route for international travellers. Search "Jeju Grande Bleu Yacht", pick your date and pay online for instant confirmation. Open the Klook listing →
  • Direct contact — for private charters, anniversaries or group bookings, message ahead with your date, group size and purpose so the team can tailor the route and timing.

Sunset times shift through the year, so the boarding time changes by season — your confirmation will list the exact meeting time.

What to wear and bring

  • Layers. It's cooler and breezier on the water than on shore, even in summer — bring a light jacket.
  • Flat, non-slip shoes. Easier and safer on deck than heels or slick soles.
  • Sunglasses and sunscreen for the low golden-hour glare.
  • A charged phone or camera. The 30 minutes before sunset is the best light — start shooting early.

Getting there

The cruise departs from Daepo Port in Seogwipo, on Jeju's south coast, beside the Jusangjeolli cliffs. From Jeju City allow roughly an hour by car or taxi; it's also reachable by local bus. For full directions and what you'll see on the water, read the Daepo Port guide.

Frequently asked questions

How long is the cruise?

The standard cruise is 1 hour. For longer, ask about a private charter.

Do I have to wear a life jacket?

Grande Bleu is the only certified yacht in Korea you can board without wearing a life jacket — but always follow the crew's on-site safety guidance.

What kind of boat is it?

A real sailing catamaran (twin hull), not a motorboat or jet boat.

Will we see dolphins?

Sometimes — they're wild, so we can't guarantee it. The coastline and the sunset are the sure things.

Is it good for kids and older travellers?

Yes. The twin hull is stable and there's an air-conditioned indoor lounge, so families and older guests are comfortable.

Can you host groups?

Absolutely — two boats hold up to 91 guests combined. See the group & charter guide.
